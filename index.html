Hi, I'm Annalisa Baronetto.

As I near the conclusion of my PhD journey, I am actively exploring roles within research and development, aiming to apply my skills and knowledge in contributing to groundbreaking advancements.

About Me

I am an AI Software Engineer specializing in wearable devices, including smart garments, and the development of AI algorithms for activity recognition and health monitoring. My expertise lies in creating and optimizing advanced algorithms to analyze data from wearable sensors, enabling accurate detection of human activities and physiological parameters. With a strong focus on healthcare, I develop innovative solutions to support telemedicine applications, such as posture analysis, strength training monitoring, and early detection of chronic disorders. By integrating technology with healthcare, I aim to enhance patient well-being and contribute to improving medical diagnostics and outcomes.

Background

I hold a B.Sc. and an M.Sc. degree in Biomedical Engineering. After graduation, I gained hands-on experience during an internship at Philips, where I coordinated post-market surveillance activities and ensured compliance with regulatory standards.

In 2019, I began my PhD in Computer Science, focusing on AI algorithms for telemedicine and smart garments. My primary focus was on innovative solutions involving smart garments, soft electronics, and additive manufacturing technology. During this phase, I gained expertise in artificial intelligence while designing AI-based algorithms to facilitate personalized wearable prototypes, tailored to individual body sizes. I also led the development and implementation of state-of-the-art methods, including deep learning models like EfficientNet and Audio Transformers, to enable the detection and classification of body sounds for health monitoring. I personally supervised clinical studies to evaluate the comfort and wearability on patients of designed prototypes, providing invaluable insights for refining my work and testing the algorithms I had developed.

Continuing my PhD journey with Hahn-Schikard after our lab's relocation to Freiburg in 2022, my focus shifted towards optimizing my deep learning models for deployment on wearable prototypes. This optimization includes model compression, demonstrating my practical orientation and dedication to making innovations ready for the real world. Furthermore, I am involved in developing AI-driven methods for the early detection of chronic digestive disorders, such as Inflammatory Bowel Disease.

Currently, I work as a Software Engineer at Panthr HealthTech, where I design algorithms for strength training monitoring using wearable sensors and smart textiles. I also develop real-time data collection platforms to enhance telemedicine applications, focusing on improving posture analysis and supporting cancer patient care. I am dedicated to push the boundaries of medical diagnostics and ultimately improving patient care.
